name: ah:research-prd
description: Research a PRD by exploring how the codebase handles related concerns, searching the web for best practices and how others solve similar problems, then giving concrete feedback on the PRD's approach. Use after writing a PRD and before slicing it into work items.
---

Given an existing PRD, research whether its proposed approach holds up.

1. **Explore the codebase** — find existing patterns, conventions, and
   code related to what the PRD proposes. Note what already exists that
   can be reused and what would need to change.

2. **Search the web** — look for how others have solved the same or
   similar problems. Focus on best practices, common pitfalls, and
   alternative approaches.

3. **Give feedback** — based on what you found, give concrete input on
   the PRD. Flag risks, suggest alternatives where the research points
   to a better approach, and confirm the parts that look solid.

Present your findings and recommendations, then ask the user how they
want to update the PRD before moving on to slicing.
